Blocksatz

I am a PhD candidate in the Regional Climate Group at the Department for Earth Sciences, University of Gothenburg. With a Master's degree in Atmospheric Sciences, I am mainly interested in the question how a warmer climate is changing atmospheric processes related to heavy rain and water availability, as these are essential for how we build our future societies. Where can we live? Where can we grow food? What are the risks for extreme events like flooding?

My PhD project is about past and future changes in the atmospheric water cycle in the Tibetan Plateau and Himalaya region with a specific focus on organized convection and extreme precipitation. My aim is to improve our understanding of the physical processes that lead to extreme precipitation, because this is a pre-requisite to robustly assess future changes in the regional climate. I also try to bridge the gap between observational and modeling perspectives, so I am working with both ground-based and space-borne observations as well as numerical weather and climate models.
